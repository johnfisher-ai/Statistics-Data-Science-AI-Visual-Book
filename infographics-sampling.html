01

Why We Sample

Chapter 61
In a nutshell
  • A census is often too costly or slow; a well-drawn sample estimates the whole from a part.
  • The error falls as SE = σ/√n, and depends on the sample size n, not the fraction of the population taken.
  • Representativeness beats size: a biased frame (Literary Digest, 1936) is wrong no matter how large.

Non-Probability Sampling

Chapter 63
In a nutshell
  • Unknown selection probabilities mean selection bias and untrustworthy error bars.
  • Bias is systematic: a convenience sample stayed +3.5 h off whether n was 100 or 20,000.
  • Post-stratification weighting can fix known imbalances (17.0 → 14.0), never unknown ones.

Determining Sample Size

Chapter 64
In a nutshell
  • For a mean: n = (z·σ/E)²; for a proportion: n = z²p(1−p)/E².
  • The required n is largest at p = 0.5, about 1,067 for a ±3% margin, the classic poll size.
  • Cost is 1/E² (halve the margin, quadruple the sample); to detect an effect you size for power.

Study Design & Data Quality

Chapter 65
In a nutshell
  • Randomization balances every confounder, so an experiment can establish causation; observation alone cannot.
  • A confounder can fake a correlation (ice cream and drownings, r = 0.58 → −0.03 once temperature is controlled).
  • Simpson's paradox: a trend can reverse when aggregated; and clean, valid, complete data is a precondition, not an afterthought.

Bias in Data Collection

Chapter 66
In a nutshell
  • Coverage, nonresponse, response, and survivorship bias all come from how data is collected, and none shrink with n.
  • Survivorship bias: the engine looked safe only because hit planes never returned, reason about the data you cannot see.
  • In ML, collection bias becomes model bias and feeds a loop (a hiring gap of 50% vs 11% was reproduced exactly).
→ Review Chapter 66
07

Modes of Data Collection

Chapter 67
In a nutshell
  • Data arrives by many modes: ask, sense, observe, log, harvest, reuse, spanning active/designed to passive/found.
  • One design framework fits all: target, frame, instrument, sampling, quality, bias check, and ethics/legality.
  • Each mode has a signature bias (sensor drift, reactivity, coverage bias), and provenance should travel with the data.
→ Review Chapter 67
08

Survey & Questionnaire Design

Chapter 68
In a nutshell
  • Operationalize a construct into items (objective → construct → dimensions → items); aim for validity and reliability.
  • Match the scale to the question (Likert, numeric/NPS, multi-select, ranking) and word items to avoid leading or double-barreled bias.
  • Structure and pretest: easy → sensitive → demographics; follow AAPOR, ESOMAR, and ISO 20252 standards.
